# Ki'bok Coffee SMA — Product Marketing Context

## Identity
Ki'bok Coffee SMA is an independent three-story cafe and espresso bar at Sollano 25, Centro, San Miguel de Allende, Mexico. Open since 2017. "Ki'bok" means "good aromas" in a Maya language from Tulum. Founded by Joel Cunningham and family. This site is standalone — not affiliated with other locations.

## Tech Stack
Next.js 16, React 19, Tailwind CSS 4, Framer Motion, next-intl (EN/ES/JA). Deployed on Netlify. All copy in src/i18n/messages/[locale].json. Menu data in src/lib/constants.ts. Images in public/ASSETS/.

## What We Offer
- 100% organic arabica from Chiapas (Daniel Ayala), Oaxaca, Veracruz (1,200-1,600m)
- Roasted by Victor Hugo Jiménez Trejo
- Signature: The Hemingway — Cuban cortado, double espresso, brown sugar, cinnamon, foam
- Other signatures: Cococcino, Oppenheimer, Latte de Olla, Buzz Latte
- Full espresso + slow bar (V60, Chemex)
- House-baked pastries, full breakfast & lunch, vegan/vegetarian/GF options
- Bean products: Ki'bok Signature Blend 2026, Café Explosión
- Three floors: espresso bar, second floor (WiFi/outlets), rooftop
- Free fast WiFi, dog-friendly
- Hours: Daily 8am-7pm. Prices in MXN ($35-$200)

## Audiences
1. Digital nomads & remote workers — WiFi, outlets, work-friendly
2. Expats & SMA residents — social gathering, morning routines
3. Tourists — "best coffee" searches, rooftop views, Instagram
4. Local Mexican customers — quality, community, artisan support

## Brand Voice
- Warm, grounded, specific — like a friend recommending their spot
- Boho-relaxed, effortlessly cool
- Sensory-first — tastes, smells, textures, light
- Bilingual-aware — English-primary, Spanish flavor (Centro, Parroquia, Sollano)
- Voice reference: VibeMeter copy ("Chilaquiles o'clock," "Golden hour — the magic window")

## Copy Rules
- NO generic platitudes ("every coffee tells a story")
- NO corporate language ("we serve with pleasure")
- NO vague claims without specifics
- ALWAYS ground in SMA — street names, landmarks
- ALWAYS elevate the food
- ALWAYS be specific — farm names, drink names, views
- When editing i18n, update ALL THREE locale files
